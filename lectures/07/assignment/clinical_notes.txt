# Sample Clinical Notes for Entity Extraction

## Note 1
Patient is a 58-year-old male presenting with chest pain radiating to the left arm. 
Blood pressure 145/92 mmHg, heart rate 88 bpm. Troponin elevated at 0.8 ng/mL.
ECG shows ST elevation in leads V1-V4. Patient started on aspirin 325mg and 
heparin drip. Diagnosis: Acute ST-elevation myocardial infarction (STEMI).

## Note 2
65-year-old female with history of type 2 diabetes presents with polyuria and 
polydipsia. Fasting glucose 285 mg/dL, HbA1c 9.2%. Current medications include 
metformin 1000mg BID and lisinopril 10mg daily. Assessment: Poorly controlled 
type 2 diabetes mellitus. Plan: Add glipizide 5mg daily, recheck HbA1c in 3 months.

## Note 3
42-year-old male with productive cough for 5 days, fever to 101.5F. Chest X-ray 
shows right lower lobe infiltrate. WBC 14,500/uL with left shift. Diagnosis: 
Community-acquired pneumonia. Started on azithromycin 500mg x1, then 250mg daily 
for 4 days. Also prescribed albuterol inhaler PRN for wheezing.

## Note 4
28-year-old female with severe headache, photophobia, and neck stiffness. 
Temperature 102.8F. Lumbar puncture performed: opening pressure elevated, 
WBC 1200 cells/uL with neutrophil predominance, glucose 35 mg/dL, protein 180 mg/dL.
Blood cultures pending. Diagnosis: Bacterial meningitis. Started on ceftriaxone 2g 
IV q12h, vancomycin 1g IV q12h, and dexamethasone 0.15 mg/kg IV q6h.
