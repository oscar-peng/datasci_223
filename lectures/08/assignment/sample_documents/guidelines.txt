# Sample Clinical Guidelines (Synthetic)

## Hypertension Management

Stage 1 hypertension is defined as systolic blood pressure 130-139 mmHg or diastolic blood pressure 80-89 mmHg. Initial treatment should include lifestyle modifications for all patients.

Lifestyle modifications include:
- Weight loss if overweight (target BMI < 25)
- DASH diet (rich in fruits, vegetables, low-fat dairy)
- Sodium restriction (< 2300 mg/day, ideally < 1500 mg/day)
- Regular physical activity (150 minutes/week moderate intensity)
- Limit alcohol consumption

First-line antihypertensive medications include:
- Thiazide diuretics (e.g., hydrochlorothiazide, chlorthalidone)
- ACE inhibitors (e.g., lisinopril, enalapril)
- ARBs (e.g., losartan, valsartan)
- Calcium channel blockers (e.g., amlodipine, nifedipine)

Choice of medication depends on comorbidities:
- Diabetes or CKD: ACE inhibitor or ARB preferred
- Heart failure: ACE inhibitor or ARB plus beta-blocker
- African American patients: Thiazide or CCB often first choice

## Diabetes Mellitus Type 2

Diagnostic criteria:
- Fasting plasma glucose >= 126 mg/dL
- HbA1c >= 6.5%
- 2-hour plasma glucose >= 200 mg/dL during OGTT
- Random glucose >= 200 mg/dL with classic symptoms

Treatment approach:
1. Lifestyle modification (diet and exercise) for all patients
2. Metformin as first-line pharmacotherapy unless contraindicated
3. Target HbA1c < 7% for most adults (individualize based on patient factors)

Metformin considerations:
- Contraindicated if eGFR < 30 mL/min/1.73m²
- Use with caution if eGFR 30-45
- Hold before contrast procedures
- Monitor for B12 deficiency with long-term use

For patients with established cardiovascular disease:
- SGLT2 inhibitors (empagliflozin, dapagliflozin) or
- GLP-1 receptor agonists (liraglutide, semaglutide)
These have proven cardiovascular benefit.

## Acute Chest Pain Evaluation

HEART Score components:
- History (highly suspicious, moderately suspicious, slightly suspicious)
- ECG (significant ST depression, nonspecific changes, normal)
- Age (>= 65, 45-64, < 45)
- Risk factors (>= 3, 1-2, none)
- Troponin (>= 3x normal, 1-3x normal, normal)

Risk stratification:
- Score 0-3: Low risk - consider discharge with follow-up
- Score 4-6: Intermediate risk - admit for observation
- Score 7-10: High risk - early invasive strategy

STEMI criteria:
- ST elevation >= 1mm in 2 contiguous leads, OR
- New left bundle branch block with ischemic symptoms
- Door-to-balloon time goal: < 90 minutes for primary PCI
- If PCI not available within 120 minutes: fibrinolysis within 30 minutes
